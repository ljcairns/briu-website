# Briu Strategy Context

## Purpose
This file captures durable strategic context for future sessions so work on the site stays aligned without re-explaining the business each time.

## Company Position
Briu is a founder-led AI implementation company building around practical AI agent deployment for businesses.

Briu is not only a consulting business. It is a services-led company with the intention to productize over time.

## Business Model
### Current model
- Founder-led consulting
- Architecture and workflow design
- Custom AI agent implementation
- Retainer-based support
- Client-specific deployments

### Future model
- Repeatable productized offerings
- Reusable internal systems that become standardized products
- SaaS or software components built from repeated patterns
- A stronger software layer alongside services

## Positioning Implications
- Do not position Briu as "just a consultant."
- Do not position Briu as a services-only business.
- Do not let the brand read like a generic agency.
- Position Briu as a premium AI implementation company that combines services, systems, and an emerging product layer.
- The site should support near-term service revenue and long-term product credibility.

## Founder Context
- Briu is emerging from two years of building Subsights.
- More recent work includes hands-on deployment with OpenClaw and AI agent systems.
- The company wants to build openly and use its own systems as proof.
- The site itself and future iterations can become content showing how Briu works.

## Audience Reality
Business owners often feel both urgency and uncertainty around AI agents.

Common emotions and concerns:
- Fear about competitors moving faster
- Uncertainty about what is real versus hype
- Concern about control, risk, cost, and governance
- Curiosity about practical adoption without chaos

Briu should address that fear with:
- Clarity
- Control
- Trust
- Strong execution

## Brand And Tone
Desired brand feel:
- Premium
- High-trust
- Thoughtful
- Founder-led
- Operationally credible
- Clear-eyed about tradeoffs

Tone guidance:
- Preserve founder voice
- Preserve substantive ideas already developed
- Reduce hype and absolutist claims
- Avoid generic "AI transformation" copy
- Make the value concrete quickly

## Homepage Direction
The homepage is currently too manifesto-heavy.

The homepage should become:
- More conversion-oriented
- Easier to understand quickly
- Stronger on trust and practical execution
- Clear about what Briu does now
- Compatible with future product credibility

The homepage should not carry the full weight of the market thesis.

## Recommended Site Structure
The site should support these top-level destinations:
1. Homepage
2. Blog / Insights
3. Why Now / Economics
4. Security / Architecture
5. Use Cases

## Content Allocation
### Homepage should do
- Explain what Briu is in plain language
- Show the service/system/product trajectory without overselling it
- Emphasize trust, control, governance, and execution
- Make it easy to start a conversation
- Show proof through founder background and Briu's own systems

### Insights should hold
- Founder essays
- Build-in-public posts
- The longer manifesto and market-thesis material
- Posts about site iterations, internal tooling, and experiments
- Commentary that shows point of view without crowding conversion pages

### Why Now / Economics should do
- Turn the strongest economic and timing arguments into a structured page
- Reduce hype and increase decision-useful clarity
- Help skeptical operators understand timing, cost curves, trust ramps, and adoption logic
- Support sales conversations without reading like a blog post

### Security / Architecture should do
- Explain deployment model, governance, approval gates, cost controls, and operational boundaries
- Reduce fear for buyers who worry about risk and oversight

### Use Cases should do
- Show concrete applied examples by workflow or business function
- Make the offer easier to picture for a buyer

## Editing Constraints
- The repo is currently a small static site.
- Prefer minimal, safe, incremental edits.
- Preserve the current design language where possible.
- Do not do a broad rewrite all at once.
- Avoid changing `index.html` until the new structure and first edit are agreed.

## Practical Guidance For Future Sessions
- Treat the homepage as a high-trust conversion surface, not the full archive of founder thinking.
- Preserve valuable existing ideas by relocating them into the right pages rather than deleting them.
- Keep the brand balanced between present-day services revenue and future software ambition.
