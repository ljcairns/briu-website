Brand in a Session

400+ Midjourney generations. Hundreds of name candidates. One logo. How agents and founder taste produced a brand identity in hours, not months.

The problem

Branding agency. 6-8 week discovery. $15,000-25,000. For a company proving agents can replace expensive outsourced work, that felt like the wrong way to start.

Finding the name

The search started in Claude. Geographic roots, Gaelic and Celtic patterns, abstract concepts, short available domains. The agent generated hundreds of candidates, each checked against domain availability, trademark risk, pronunciation, and brand fit.

We built an OpenClaw skill mid-session to automate domain search: whois lookups, .com and .ai availability, trademark screening through USPTO. 50+ candidates got the full treatment.

"Briu" came from Catalan. The word works two ways: as a noun meaning energy, push, courage (synonyms include energia, empenta, coratge, ànim) and as an interjection used to urge action, like "let's go."

Briu isn't a word you'd hear in a Barcelona coffee shop. It's older than that. In modern street Catalan, people say empenta for drive or fot-li canya for step on it. But briu persists in the literary tradition, in the northern Rossellonès dialect where it's still spoken naturally, and in its cousin languages: "brio" in Italian and English, "brío" in Spanish. It traces back to Proto-Celtic roots meaning strength. We chose it because it carries weight without shouting. Energy, push, courage. A Catalan word that led us to Gaudi, Barcelona, and the visual language that became our brand.

The .ai was available. No trademark conflicts. Short, pronounceable, didn't sound like every other AI company. The name did something else we didn't plan for. A Catalan word naturally pointed toward Barcelona, and Barcelona pointed toward Gaudi. The name and the design language share the same root.

Cost: a few dollars in API across 50+ candidates. $85 for the domain.

The visual identity

The same Claude thread that generated names was also generating Midjourney prompts. It had the context (the Catalan origin, the Gaudi connection, the brand personality) so we used it to write the prompts, and then screenshotted the results back into the thread for selection. The loop was: Claude writes prompts, Midjourney generates a batch, founder screenshots the results back, Claude and founder both pick favorites (often the same ones), and that feeds the next round of prompts.

The first runs were wide open. B lettermarks, flames, arrows, chevrons, abstract symbols, stars. Everything on the table. Claude was prompting across dozens of directions at once to map the space.

Then the arch appeared. Clean gold line-art arches on black. Concentric, nested, pointed. Mountains and trees kept getting tested alongside, but the arch kept winning every round. We pushed hard on variations: line weight, symmetry, layering, pointed vs. rounded, with and without a base.

Once the arch locked in, the Gaudi influence came back in. We layered mosaic texture onto the arch form. Teal, copper, gold tilework inspired by Sagrada Familia and Park Guell. This phase had the longest iteration run. Dozens of near-identical generations with subtle differences in color balance, arch proportion, and texture density.

Final mark: a Gaudi-inspired mosaic arch, assembled in Canva. Proportions, dark background testing, 16px favicon to full-page hero.

400+ generations. One evening.

What surprised us

Claude could actually pick winners. When we screenshotted a batch of generations back into the thread, it identified the strongest candidates, and generally picked the same ones the founder would have. It could articulate why: "that one works as a brand mark because the form is distinctive at small sizes" or "the arch reads better than the tree because it has more inherent symmetry." In the early phases, most generations are junk, so the selection is obvious. But even as the iterations got tighter, Claude was picking for the right reasons.

What the agent couldn't do was set the direction. It couldn't decide that the arch was more meaningful than the mountain for this brand. It couldn't feel when the mosaic texture went from "interesting" to "right." The founder made every strategic call. Which threads to pursue, when to abandon a direction, when to push deeper. The agent wrote the prompts, evaluated the results, and kept the iteration loop moving fast. That loop ran hundreds of times in one evening.

The takeaway

One creative arc. The agent searched hundreds of names across multiple languages and landed on a Catalan word. That word pointed to a visual world. Gaudi, Barcelona, arches, mosaics. The founder directed Midjourney through 400+ iterations refining that visual world into a clean mark, then finalized it in Canva. Name to logo, orchestrated by a human using agents at every step.

The $120 brand is live at briu.ai right now.
