Creating the Briu Brand with Agents

400+ Midjourney generations. Hundreds of name candidates. One logo. How agents and founder taste produced a brand identity in hours, not months.

The problem

Branding agency. 6-8 week discovery. $15,000-25,000. For a company proving agents can replace expensive outsourced work, that felt like the wrong way to start.

Finding the name

The search started in Claude. Geographic roots, Gaelic and Celtic patterns, abstract concepts, short available domains. The agent generated hundreds of candidates, each checked against domain availability, trademark risk, pronunciation, and brand fit.

We built an OpenClaw skill mid-session to automate domain search: whois lookups, .com and .ai availability, trademark screening through USPTO. 50+ candidates got the full treatment.

"Briu" came from Catalan. The word works two ways: as a noun meaning energy, push, courage — synonyms include energia, empenta, coratge, ànim — and as an interjection used to urge action, like "let's go."

Briu is not a word you'd hear in a Barcelona coffee shop. It's older than that. In modern street Catalan, people say empenta for drive or fot-li canya for step on it. But briu persists in the literary tradition, in the northern Rossellonès dialect where it's still spoken naturally, and in its cousin languages: "brio" in Italian and English, "brío" in Spanish. It traces back to Proto-Celtic roots meaning strength. We chose it because it carries weight without shouting. Energy, push, courage. A Catalan word that led us to Gaudi, Barcelona, and the visual language that became our brand.

The .ai was available. No trademark conflicts. Short, pronounceable, didn't sound like every other AI company. The name did something else we didn't plan for — a Catalan word naturally pointed toward Barcelona, and Barcelona pointed toward Gaudi. The name and the design language share the same root.

Cost: a few dollars in API across 50+ candidates. $85 for the domain.

The visual identity

With the Catalan origin pointing toward Gaudi, we had a starting prompt. Midjourney runs referenced his aesthetic: the pointed arches of Sagrada Familia, the mosaic tilework of Park Guell, organic geometry with warm stone and gold.

First runs leaned hard into that world. Mosaic arches, heavy texture, teal and copper and gold on dark backgrounds. Beautiful but too complex for a logo — intricate stonework and ornamental detail that would disappear at small sizes.

The Gaudi influence evolved from literal mosaic into clean geometric arches. Texture dropped away, DNA stayed: the pointed arch, warm gold palette, something architectural and layered. Gold on black. Concentric arches, pointed peaks, nested shapes suggesting architecture and mountains.

Divergent phase. Trees, flames, mountain peaks, compass roses, Celtic knots, mosaic B's, geometric B's. Some stunning as standalone art, wrong as logos.

Convergence. The arch motif kept winning. Line weight, symmetry, layering, pointed vs. rounded, with and without a base. Dozens of near-identical generations with tiny differences.

Final mark: clean, layered arch in warm gold tones. Assembled in Canva — proportions, dark background testing, 16px favicon to full-page hero.

400+ generations. One evening.

What the agent cannot do here

The agent generated 400+ images. It could not tell which were good. It could not decide that the arch was more meaningful than the mountain, or that clean gold line art said more than textured mosaic.

Every generation was a response to a human prompt. Every prompt informed by the previous generation. Human taste directs, agent expands the search space, human selects, agent refines. That loop ran hundreds of times in one evening.

The takeaway

One creative arc. The agent searched hundreds of names across multiple languages and landed on a Catalan word. That word pointed to a visual world — Gaudi, Barcelona, arches, mosaics. The founder directed Midjourney through 400+ iterations refining that visual world into a clean mark, then finalized it in Canva. Name to logo, orchestrated by a human using agents at every step.

The $120 brand is live at briu.ai right now.
