The traditional cost of automation

When a business wants to automate workflows internally, the standard path is to hire. An AI/ML engineer, a data engineer, a project manager. Even a modest team costs six figures per month before they ship a single workflow.

The subscription model flips this. Instead of building a team, you subscribe to outcomes. Same workflows, fraction of the cost, deployed in weeks instead of quarters.

What you don't pay for

The comparison table only captures direct costs. The hidden costs of hiring are where the real gap lives.

  • Recruiting time 3–6 months to hire an AI engineer. Your automation waits while you interview.
  • Ramp-up period New hires need 2–4 months to understand your stack, your data, your processes.
  • Retention risk AI talent has a median tenure of 18 months. When they leave, institutional knowledge leaves with them.
  • Management overhead Someone has to manage, review, and unblock. That's your time or another hire.
  • Tooling & infra Dev environments, CI/CD, monitoring, model hosting. Costs that compound before anything ships.
  • Opportunity cost Every month spent building a team is a month your competitors are deploying agents.

When hiring does make sense

This isn't a blanket argument against building internal AI capability. If you have 10+ workflows to automate, a proprietary model to fine-tune, or AI is your core product differentiator — you should hire. Briu is for the 90% of businesses where AI is a tool, not the product. We get you running while you decide whether to build the team later.
