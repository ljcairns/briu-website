Why Now / Economics

A structured case for acting early without acting recklessly.

This page holds the deeper argument behind timing, trust, and economics. The goal is not to sell a fantasy of full autonomy. It is to explain why careful early adoption can matter operationally before the category settles.

What This Page Will Cover

Trust ramps

How sensible deployments move from read-only assistance to approved actions and only later to selective automation.

Cost curves

Why model quality and price trends change the economics over time, and why the first deployment does not need to mirror the final one.

Operational learning

Why the durable advantage is often process knowledge, internal systems, and governance habits rather than a flashy demo.

Briu is building this argument from implementation work in the field. Services work creates the pattern library. Over time, the most repeatable parts can become productized systems and software.

Economics

Cheaper and better, every quarter.

The most important point is not a single price number. It is the direction of the curve. Frontier capability keeps rising while the cost of useful deployment keeps falling, which changes the economics of starting now versus waiting for a supposedly perfect later moment.

Projections based on observed 50%+ annual decline in frontier pricing 2023-2026.

The question is not whether every business should run at full autonomous capacity immediately. It is whether starting with a supervised, lower-risk deployment today creates learning and leverage before the economics become obvious to everyone else.
