# MarsChat Brand Strategy

## 1. Domain Candidates

Priority order based on availability likelihood, brand strength, and project fit.

| Priority | Domain | Trademark Viability | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------------------|-------|
| 1 | lightlag.io | **Strong** — coined/fanciful term, no existing use | Top pick. Unique, memorable, directly evokes the core concept (light-speed delay). No trademark conflicts expected. |
| 2 | lightlag.com | **Strong** — same coined term | .com carries more mainstream weight. Likely parked or available at premium. Worth checking. |
| 3 | lightlag.ai | **Strong** — same coined term | .ai TLD signals tech/AI angle. Good if protocol simulation is the lead positioning. |
| 4 | farcast.io | **Moderate** — suggestive mark | Suggests "casting far" which is evocative but not unique. Risk: Farcaster (crypto social protocol) creates adjacency confusion. |
| 5 | farcast.com | **Moderate** — suggestive mark | Same concerns as farcast.io. Likely taken or premium given Farcaster ecosystem. |
| 6 | voidlink.io | **Weaker** — descriptive/compound | Two common words joined. Harder to defend as trademark. Sounds more like a VPN or privacy tool than a space comms project. |
| 7 | arcsend.io | **Moderate** — suggestive | "Arc" + "send" is evocative but generic-leaning. Less immediately tied to the Mars delay concept. |

**Recommendation:** Secure lightlag.io first, then lightlag.com if budget allows. The coined nature of "lightlag" gives the strongest trademark position and the most distinctive brand.

## 3. Waitlist Conversion Strategy

### Phase 1: MVP (0–500 subscribers)

- **Tool:** Formspree form embedded on lightlag landing page
- **Fields:** Email only (reduce friction)
- **Trigger:** "Get notified when the simulation goes live"
- **Data:** Formspree submissions export to CSV for migration

### Phase 2: Scale (500+ subscribers)

- **Migrate to:** Buttondown (indie-friendly, simple, affordable)
- **Why Buttondown:** No tracking pixels by default, markdown emails, fair pricing, aligns with the project's tone
- **Migration:** CSV import from Formspree, deduplicate, send welcome-back email

### Email Sequence

1. **Welcome** (immediate) — "You're on the list. Here's what lightlag is and why the delay matters."
2. **Protocol Explainer** (day 3) — "How we simulate light-speed delay and what it reveals about communication design."
3. **Simulation Link** (on launch) — "It's live. Try a conversation with 14-minute lag."
4. **Briu.ai Case Study** (day 7 post-launch) — "How we built this for under $1K — the full breakdown on briu.ai/experiments."

### Metrics to Track

- Waitlist signups per week
- Email open rate (Buttondown provides this)
- Simulation activation rate (signed up → actually tried it)
- Referral source (which influencer mention drove signups)

## 4. Briu.ai Attribution

Lightlag is an experiment by Briu. The connection should be visible but not dominant.

### Footer Link

Every lightlag page includes a subtle footer line:

> An experiment by [briu.ai](https://briu.ai)

### Waitlist CTA Crossover

- Lightlag waitlist subscribers get the briu.ai case study email (sequence step 4)
- Briu.ai newsletter (if/when created) mentions lightlag as a live experiment
- No cross-pollination of lists without opt-in

### Case Study on Briu.ai

- URL: `briu.ai/build/lightlag` (or `/experiments/lightlag`)
- Content: full build log — cost breakdown, domain choice, protocol design decisions, influencer outreach results
- Links back to lightlag.io for the live simulation
- Demonstrates Briu's "build fast, build cheap, build real" positioning

### Attribution Rules

- Lightlag is branded independently (its own visual identity, not Briu's color scheme)
- Briu connection is "by" credit, not co-branding
- If lightlag gains traction, it validates Briu's model without diluting either brand

## 5. Exit Strategy

Lightlag is designed to be acquirable as a standalone asset package.

### What's Acquirable

- **Brand:** lightlag name, domain(s), visual identity, social accounts
- **Waitlist:** Opted-in subscriber list with engagement history
- **Protocol IP:** Delay simulation protocol, message queue architecture, UX patterns for high-latency communication
- **Content:** All published articles, influencer relationships, case studies

### Target Acquirers

| Acquirer | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **SpaceX** | Starlink already handles Earth comms. Mars comms is a known future need. Lightlag is a ready-made UX research asset. |
| **Relativity Space** | Building rockets for Mars missions. Comms protocol research adds to their mission stack. |
| **NASA Commercial Partners** | JPL already deals with deep-space delay. A consumer-facing simulation tool has education and outreach value. |

### Exit Conditions

- Waitlist > 5,000 with >30% open rates = demonstrable audience
- Protocol simulation publicly live and functional = working product
- Influencer coverage with measurable referral traffic = distribution proof
- Clean IP (no dependencies on proprietary APIs, no GPL entanglements)

### Valuation Basis

The asset is small but novel. Value comes from:
1. First-mover brand in "Mars communication UX"
2. Engaged niche audience (space enthusiasts are high-intent)
3. Protocol design work that would cost an acquirer 6+ months to replicate
4. Ready-made marketing narrative ("we acquired the team that simulated Mars chat")
