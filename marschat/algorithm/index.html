Interactive Walkthrough — Lightlag Protocol

The algorithm that turns latency into a planning problem.

Lightlag does not try to outrun the speed of light. It bundles likely next moves ahead of the hop, validates that bundle before transmission, and then uses receiver corrections to improve the next round. The objective is fewer round trips, not bigger messages for their own sake.

03 — Correction Loop

Receiver edits are not failure. They are training data for the next bundle.

Lightlag improves when the receiver marks what was right, what was missing, and what should be rewritten. The next expansion round should cover more of the conversation with fewer corrections.

Iterative Convergence
Round 1 expansions > corrections > Round 2 improves > corrections decrease > convergence
04 — Compression Metric

Latency saved depends on how many exchanges the bundle resolves before the next hop.

This calculator uses a default Lightlag assumption of five effective exchanges resolved per bundled round, matching the protocol example used across the concept page.

Formula
Compression by fewer round trips
T_without = E × RTT
T_with = ceil(E ÷ 5) × RTT
Compression = T_without ÷ T_with

E is the number of conversational exchanges required without AI assistance. RTT is round-trip delay in minutes. The constant 5 is the estimated bundle yield: one Lightlag round resolves about five ordinary exchanges before the next physical hop is needed.
